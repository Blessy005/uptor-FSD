  1. Hacksaw Ridge (2016)

    2. Hacksaw Ridge is a 2016 war film based on the true story of Desmond Doss, an American combat medic who became the first conscientious objector to be awarded the Medal of Honor without firing a single shot. During the Battle of Okinawa in World War II, Doss saved 75 men by single-handedly carrying them to safety while under enemy fire. The movie, directed by Mel Gibson, portrays his struggles with his pacifist beliefs in the face of military opposition and the horrors of war.

  2. Fury (2014)

    3. Fury is a 2014 World War II film about a battle-hardened American tank crew fighting their way through Germany in the final weeks of the war. Written and directed by David Ayer, the movie stars Brad Pitt as the crew's leader, "Wardaddy," and portrays the brutal and psychologically tolling experiences of soldiers in their Sherman tank. The story follows the crew as they are joined by an inexperienced soldier and face overwhelming odds against a superior German force.

  3. Oppenheimer (2023)

    4. Oppenheimer is a 2023 biographical thriller film directed by Christopher Nolan that chronicles the life of physicist J. Robert Oppenheimer. The movie focuses on his pivotal role in the development of the atomic bomb during World War II as head of the Los Alamos Laboratory for the Manhattan Project, the complex moral and ethical dilemmas he faced, and the subsequent fallout from his creation of the weapon. The film explores Oppenheimer's mind, ambition, and the guilt that haunted him, culminating in his 1954 security hearing.

  4. 12 Angry Men (1957)

    5. 12 Angry Men is a 1957 American legal drama about a jury deliberating the fate of a young man accused of murder. Set almost entirely in a single jury room on a hot New York afternoon, the film follows the twelve jurors as they are forced to re-examine evidence and confront their own prejudices after one juror initially votes "not guilty". The story builds suspense through their escalating arguments as they work toward a unanimous decision, which could either send an innocent man to his death or a guilty one to freedom.

  5. The Pianist (2002)

    6. The Pianist is a 2002 biographical war film directed by Roman Polanski, based on the autobiography of Polish-Jewish pianist Władysław Szpilman. The movie depicts Szpilman's struggle for survival in Warsaw after the city is invaded by the Nazis during World War II, detailing his harrowing experiences hiding in the ruins and enduring the occupation.
