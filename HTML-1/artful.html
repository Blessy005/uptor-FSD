Artful/Cinematic Classics

Films that are visually stunning, narratively rich, and emotionally resonant — masterpieces that linger in the mind long after the credits roll.

  1. Parasite (2019)

    2. Parasite is a 2019 South Korean black comedy-thriller about the impoverished Kim family, who scheme their way into the lives of the wealthy Park family, posing as unrelated, skilled workers. The film, directed by Bong Joon-ho, uses this infiltration to explore themes of class disparity and social inequality in a story that shifts from dark comedy to tragedy.

  2. Drive (2011)

    3. The name "Drive" can refer to two different movies: the 2011 American film starring Ryan Gosling, a stunt driver who moonlights as a getaway driver, gets involved in a heist that goes wrong, and must protect his neighbor and her son. The 2019 Indian Hindi-language film, starring Sushant Singh Rajput, is an action-heist film about a street racing gang trying to catch a thief.

  3. Once Upon a Time in Hollywood (2019)

    4. Once Upon a Time in Hollywood is Quentin Tarantino's 2019 period comedy-drama film set in 1969 Los Angeles, following fading TV actor Rick Dalton (Leonardo DiCaprio) and his stunt double Cliff Booth (Brad Pitt) as they navigate the changing film industry and navigate their lives near the infamous Manson Family. The film features the real-life figures Sharon Tate and Roman Polanski living next door to Dalton, weaving fictional storylines with historical events to create a "rage fantasy" exploring how things could have gone differently on the night of the Tate murders.

  4. Arrival (2016)

    5. Arrival is a 2016 science fiction film about linguist Louise Banks, played by Amy Adams, who is recruited to lead an elite team to communicate with extraterrestrial visitors. When 12 alien spacecraft land around the world, Banks and physicist Ian Donnelly (Jeremy Renner) race against time to decipher the aliens' complex language, trying to prevent global panic and war while uncovering a deeper truth about communication, time, and the human condition. The film, directed by Denis Villeneuve, explores themes of language, perception, and connection.

    More [IMDb Link]


  5. Taxi Driver (1976)

    6. Taxi Driver is a 1976 American neo-noir psychological drama about Travis Bickle, a lonely Vietnam War veteran who becomes a taxi driver in a decaying 1970s New York City. His insomnia and disgust with the city's "filth" lead him to descend into a state of paranoia and violence as he becomes obsessed with cleaning up the streets. The film follows his deteriorating mental state and his violent attempts to save a teenage prostitute and assassinate a presidential candidate.

  6. The Northman (2022)

    7. The Northman is an epic 2022 film about a Viking prince, Amleth, who embarks on a quest for revenge after his uncle murders his father, usurps the throne, and abducts his mother. The film is a brutal and visually stunning historical action drama based on the legend of Amleth, which inspired Shakespeare's Hamlet. It stars Alexander Skarsgård, Nicole Kidman, Claes Bang, and Anya Taylor-Joy, and is directed by Robert Eggers.

  7. The Big Short (2015) [NOT EVERYONE'S TASTE]

    8. The Big Short is a 2015 Oscar-winning film that tells the true story of the outsiders who predicted and profited from the 2008 financial crisis by betting against the U.S. housing market. The movie, based on a book by Michael Lewis, follows several key players—including hedge-fund manager Mark Baum (Steve Carell), investor Michael Burry (Christian Bale), and banker Jared Vennett (Ryan Gosling)—as they navigate the dark underbelly of modern banking and realize that a massive economic collapse is imminent.
